Who is Harald Lønsethagen?

As you might already know, my name is Harald Lønsethagen. I'm 23 years old and live in Norway where I study at NTNU. I'm pationate about computers and have developed a love for programming.

I love technology and like to test out new gadgets. From a young age I played alot with LEGO and loved building bigger and more complex structures. My family mainly consist of engineers, so it came as no suprise when I decided to study for a masters-degree in electroengineering. My mindset has always been fixed towards solving the complex problems. My mind often drift away when I stirr and try to figure out how to solve the latest challange which introded my mind.

My latest challange has been connected with my latest large project. I've recently joined a student organization called Revolve NTNU. Revolve is each year building a new electrical race-car competing in the biggest student-engineering-competition in the world. Each year hundrets of teams from around the world gather in Hockenheim Germany and try to show of their newest racing-car, both in dynamic events, where the car is racing at a track, but also in static events where the team has to present their engineering-decisions and defend every little concept they have used. But now for the exciting part. In 2017 Formula Student Germany (FSG) hosted a new competition class called Driverless Vehicle. Here, you take a previously build racing-car by your student organization and make it drive fully autonomously. This introduces a heck load of new challanges since driverless vehicles, and especially autonomously-racing vehicles has not been tested anywhere near as much as 'regular' vehicles. This is, as you might share, an extremly cool challange.
